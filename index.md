## 🛠️ Getting the best results

HDR photography requires a specific workflow to get the best output. Follow these tips to improve your images.

*   Take your photos on a tripod. Moving your camera between shots creates alignment issues.
*   Shoot in RAW format. RAW files contain more data than JPEG files.
*   Use a wide range of exposure values. Take at least three shots: one for highlights, one for mid-tones, and one for shadows.
*   Use the "Deghosting" feature inside the software. This feature aligns objects that moved between your shots.
*   Preview your changes in real-time. The interface allows you to see how adjustments affect your photo instantly.
*   Save your final image in a high-quality format like TIFF or PNG to retain your edits.
